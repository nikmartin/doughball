Recipe Settings

The default recipe is a great general purpose recipe, but you may want to tweak it to suit your tastes. This calculator does have its limits though, so we only allow settings that are considered "valid" for any style of pizza dough.

Hydration: {{settings.hydration}}%

50 80
This will be a loose, moist dough, so have plently of flour available. Works well in a conventional oven so as to not dry out too much. This will be a drier, easy to handle dough. It will be slightly harder to stretch, but a rolling pin may help. The default of 60% is great for home ovens. The extra moisture prevents a dry crust. 65% is perfect for super hot pizza ovens.

Yeast: {{settings.yeast}}%

.25 2
For a slow rise over 24-48 hours, use .25-.5% yeast. In a hurry or started your dough late? Go for 1%-1.25%. A yeast packet is 7 grams, just use it all if close.

Salt: {{settings.salt}}%

.5 4
Salt is flavor, but as you increase, you retard your yeast. We like 2.5% Keep it under 2% if you're in a hurry

Olive Oil: {{settings.oo}}%

0 3
Oil is completely optional, but we like it around 1.5% in our dough. EVOO is the best! Add it after the flour has been fully hydrated, or the last minute or two in the mixer or while kneading. Oil adds softness and flavor to the finished crust.
